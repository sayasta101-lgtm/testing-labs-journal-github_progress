# Part 5 – Final Reflection
Write **200–300 words** describing your learning experience during this lab.
Consider:
* What surprised you about computer hardware?
* What components were new to you?
* What challenges did you face?
* What did you learn about embedded systems?
  During this lab, I learned a lot about computer hardware and how different components work together inside a system. One of the most interesting parts was identifying the internal components of a computer after opening the case. Seeing parts like the motherboard, CPU, RAM, storage drive, and cooling system made me understand that a computer is not just a single device but a combination of many small, important parts working together.

What surprised me most was how small and complex some components are. The CPU, for example, is very tiny but extremely powerful, handling millions of instructions per second. I also didn’t expect the motherboard to be so detailed, with so many slots, chips, and connectors all arranged so precisely. Another surprise was how important cooling is. I used to think fans were simple accessories, but I now understand they are essential for preventing overheating and keeping the system stable.

I also learned how integrated modern systems are. Devices like the Raspberry Pi combine many functions into one board, which makes them compact and efficient. This was surprising because I previously thought computers always had separate parts for everything.

Overall, this lab helped me connect theory with real hardware. Instead of just reading about components, I could actually see how they are physically arranged and connected. It improved my understanding of how data flows between parts and how software relies on hardware to function properly. I now feel more confident in identifying computer components and understanding their roles in a complete system.
